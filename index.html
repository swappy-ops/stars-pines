Why people come

Not the same
reason twice.

01

The silence

At 1,645 metres, sound behaves differently. The valley muffles it. Wind carries it away. You hear your own thoughts more clearly here — which is either exactly what you needed or the last thing you expected.

02

The light

Mountain light is not like other light. At this altitude, at this latitude — something about the quality of the early morning, the long afternoon, the pink hour before dark — it makes you want to photograph everything and then put your phone away.

03

The company

People who come to Crank's Ridge are a specific type. Not tourists. Not people killing time. Writers with deadlines they've been ignoring. Travellers mid-transition. People who read. Conversations here tend to be worth having.

A day here

The ridge
sets the rhythm.

5:45Morning

The terrace, before anyone else

On clear days, October through March especially, the Nanda Devi range appears at this hour and no other. The peaks emerge slowly from dark. You will not regret waking up for this.

8:00Morning

Breakfast and the plan

Tea, eggs, the day spread out in front of you. No programme. The unmarked ridge trail starts at the gate. The temple is ten minutes away. Or nothing at all — the common room, a book, the view from the window.

10:00Morning

Into the ridge

Ask at the desk for the trail that's not on any map. It takes about two hours. Ends somewhere with a view that'll make you want to stay another night. Most people ask about extending their stay on the trail, before they've even returned.

2:00Afternoon

The long afternoon

Mountain afternoons are long here and still. The light slows. This is reading time, writing time, or simply sitting time. The ridge has no opinion on which.

5:00Evening

The light changes

Mountain light at dusk is the ridge's most generous hour. The peaks go pink, then orange, then deep. This is when most guests stop pretending they're going to leave tomorrow.

7:00Evening

Fire and conversation

The outdoor firepit. Whoever's staying. Stories from the road, books compared, music someone has on their phone. The evening finds its own shape.

11:00Night

The reason you came

Lie on your back on the terrace. No phone. Let your eyes adjust. The Milky Way is not metaphorical from here. The ridge has been doing this for everyone who came before you.

The house

A place built
for the ridge,
not a product.

Stars & Pines started the way good mountain places do — from knowing what this ridge feels like and wanting others to feel it too. Not from hospitality training. From the ridge itself. From the particular quality of silence at 1,645 metres that makes you want to build something honest around it.

The house is honest. Not designed to impress. Designed to get out of the way of the mountains, and to make sure that when you wake up, the peaks are the first thing you see and the warmth of the space is the second.

Your name is known before check-in. A trail you won't find on Google is waiting to be recommended. And when you ask about extending your stay — and you will ask — the availability has already been checked.

This is not a hostel. This is a mountain house with beds.

Getting here

"The journey is half of it. The last stretch, from the plains into the Kumaon hills — that's where the altitude starts to work on you."

By air: Pantnagar Airport, 115 km. Taxi up through the hills — 3.5 hrs.

By rail: Kathgodam or Haldwani. Shatabdi from Delhi in ~5.5 hrs. Taxi from station, 3–3.5 hrs.

By road: Delhi to Almora via NH9, ~8h 45min. Long. Beautiful once you leave the plains.

Address: Kasar Devi Road, Almora, Uttarakhand — 263601
