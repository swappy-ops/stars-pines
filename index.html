The Ridge Through Time

A living timeline of Crank's Ridge and Kasar Devi — from a small temple to a home for seekers.
1920s

Swami Vivekanand on the Ridge

Swami Vivekanand meditated at Kasar Devi, drawn by the profound silence and the Himalayan vista. The ridge was already known as a place of spiritual power.

1930s–60s

The Temple Grows

What began as a small shrine gradually grew. The Kasar Devi temple became a landmark — visible from across the ridge. A sketch shows how it once stood alone against the mountain.

Later Life

Shunyata Baba

Shunyata Baba built his home on the ridge in his later life. His samadhi still exists here — a quiet resting point for a man who sought silence. Krishnamurthy Ji also spent time on these slopes.

Today

7+ years of travellers finding home. A mountain house where the ridge sets the rhythm and the stars keep time.
